Price is usually the first question and the hardest to get a straight answer to, because most vendors hide it behind a sales call. So here it is plainly, with the ranges up front and the reasoning after.

## What drives the cost

Four things move the price more than anything else:

- **Users and entities.** More people and more legal entities cost more.
- **Feature depth.** Basic work papers are cheaper than a full suite with analytics and risk management.
- **Support and methodology.** Software alone is cheaper than software paired with audit expertise, but the second is often better value.
- **Buyer profile.** Tools aimed at large enterprises are priced for them, whoever is buying.

## Enterprise GRC versus mid-market tools

Enterprise governance, risk and compliance (GRC) platforms are powerful and expensive, built for large institutions with the budget and the team to run them. Mid-market internal audit tools do the core job, planning, work papers, findings, remediation and board reporting, at a fraction of the cost. For a SACCO, fintech or NGO, the mid-market tool is almost always the right fit, because you pay for what you use.

## What to look for beyond price

The cheapest tool that does not fit your workflow is expensive. Check that it covers how you actually work, keeps evidence secure, and comes with onboarding and support.
